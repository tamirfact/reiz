PERPETUAL CALENDAR

In 1987, MoMA commissioned Dan Reisinger to create a prepetual calendar. Reisinger has creared a vibrant and interactive calendar that empowered users to form up to 46,080 visually appealing combinations of color and form. The original design consisted of a durable metal frame holding six double-sided, colored cutout sheets and date cards. This digital interactive version builds upon Reisinger's concept, offering new ways to engage with his work that were unavailable in the original physical format.





Original design by Dan Reisinger.

Digital version and concept by Nadav Barkan and Tamir Pomerantz.
